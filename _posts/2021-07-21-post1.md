# Notes on General Probability Theory

For uncountably infinite sample spaces, the probability of any particular outcome is zero. Therefore, we must define probabilities of events directly. We use probability measure.   

Let $\Omega$ be a non-empty set and let F be a collection of subsets of $\Omega$. $\implies$ F is a $\sigma$-algebra (also called $\sigma$-field) given that:  
* the empty set $\phi$ belongs to F
* whenever a set A belongs to F, its complement A<sup>c</sup> also belongs to F
* whenever a sequence of sets A<sub>1</sub>, A<sub>2</sub> ... belongs to F, their union $\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}$ also belongs to F
